DATA1100: Digital cities and the welfare state

28. september 2023

The situation is about creating a “perfect” city where everyone will ble able to afford casual life, by paying the price of giving away personal information. Building an entire neighborhood by digital technology infrastructure, including machine learning algorithms, means that people living in the part of the city are going to be exposed to a constant and permanent monitoring and surveillance by a lot of sensors and cameras. The goal of creating a digital city would be to optimize every aspect of a “normal” city, which means improvements in many different aspects of our daily lives. This case is related to our profession in the way that we are the ones that will work on implementing many of these changes. We are kind of going to be the pioneers of the digital cities.

Immediate reactions

It is scary to think that our private lives might be endangered Will there be a split in the society? What future do digital cities bring to our lives and how will our roles alter in the society? Is it a waste of time and resources? Are we able to make it work in order to prevent the waste? How do we choose who lives in those cities, who is eligible and who is not? Is it going to be completely digital, only machines that run things around? Will gathering a huge amount of data from different sources be more positive or negative for all individuals who live in those cities?

Three potentially positive aspects of digital cities

  • Gathering and analyzing lot of information from different sources such as sensors and smartphones, will make it possible to efficient public resources, give possible best solutions to problems, which in turn will save resources and develop new ideas.
  • More people will be able to get housing, especially now when the prices for an apartment are so high. That will give higher quality of life and effective lifestyle.
  • Using information gathered from individuals will increase efficiency in providing services to individuals, in many different areas of individual lives.

    • Three potentially negative aspects of digital cities

  • It is scary to think that someone is going to have such a free pass to your personal information. Who will be able to see and manage this information, only machines or is there someone else?
  • Will this have any outcome on the environment? How will digital cities “act” on the nature? Is there going to be more or less waste, and how is the disposal of the waste going to look like?
  • The split between developed/rich countries and the less developed/poor countries might become even bigger?

    • Our opinions

    We believe that digital cities might make the societyand the city worse, and here is why: One one side, the idea of a digital “perfectly” optimized city sounds great. Improved health care, better services and probably less crime as well as accidents. The gathered information might be used to do good, and would maybe improve our lives. On the other side, the negative aspects outweigh the positive ones. Our private lives would be greatly compromised and endangered if something went wrong and someone foul got a hold of sensitive information. Another problem is that we minimize the social aspects of our lives. If everything is runned by robots, we will lose the interactions between humans that occur in simple services as shops. Digital cities will then kinda dehumanize us, since it will reduce interactions.
